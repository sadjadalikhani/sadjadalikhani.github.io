I wrapped up an amazing summer with the Applied Research Lab at Nokia Bell Labs. My internship focused on creating a flexible, multi-modal wireless data generation pipeline that links ray-tracing data, digital twin metadata, and baseband signals. The resulting datasets powered several pretraining runs of large wireless models for environment-aware communication and sensing tasks.

#### Highlights

- Designed a modular orchestration engine that produces synchronized channel, sensing, and control logs for city-scale digital twins.
- Benchmarked sparse spatio-temporal attention blocks for channel prediction against classical baselines.
- Collaborated with researchers across the Murray Hill and Espoo sites on evaluation scripts that we plan to open source.

Many of these insights are now feeding into the LWM-Temporal and LWM-Spectro projects. Stay tuned for preprints and demos!
